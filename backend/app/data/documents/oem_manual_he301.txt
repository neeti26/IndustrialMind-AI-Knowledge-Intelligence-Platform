3. INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Shell inspection per ASME PCC-2 — maximum 12 months between inspections
3.2 Tube bundle pull-out inspection — every 3 years or following any abnormal
    pressure differential (>10% above design DP)
3.3 Flange gasket inspection — every 6 months for NH3 service (OISD-116 Sec 18.2)
3.4 Wall thickness measurement by UT (ultrasonic testing) — 20% sample each year
3.5 MINIMUM WALL THICKNESS ALERT: Original tube wall 2.11 mm. Calculated minimum
    allowable wall at design pressure: 1.45 mm. If any tube measurement falls
    below 1.6 mm, take tube out of service immediately.

4. OPERATING LIMITS
Normal Operating Pressure (shell): 38–42 kg/cm²g
Normal Operating Temperature (shell inlet): 145–155°C
Maximum allowable NH3 leakage rate: zero tolerance — any detected leak requires
immediate work order per OISD-116 Section 7 and plant LOTO procedure.

5. KNOWN FAILURE MODES
5.1 Flange gasket leakage — primary failure mode for this service (RTJ gaskets
    susceptible to relaxation under thermal cycling). Inspection interval CRITICAL.
5.2 Tube sheet cracking — documented in 2021 (repaired under WO-2021-0445).
    Monitor with PT/MT testing during each shutdown.
5.3 Inlet nozzle erosion — increased risk following 2022 upsizing due to higher
    velocity. Inspect nozzle bore at each shutdown.
5.4 Waterside fouling — cooling tower water hardness causes calcium carbonate
    deposits on tube ID. Chemical cleaning required if tube-side DP increases >15%.

7. EMERGENCY ISOLATION
Emergency isolation: Close XV-301A (shell side block valve) and XV-301B (tube side block valve).
NH3 vent to flare via EV-301 (pneumatic vent valve, fail-open on loss of instrument air).
Location of block valves: See P&ID-VFC-A3-REV7, Sheet 3.
