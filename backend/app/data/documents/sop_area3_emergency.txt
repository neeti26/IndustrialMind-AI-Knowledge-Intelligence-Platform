STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE — EMERGENCY RESPONSE
SOP No: SOP-VFC-EMERG-A3-REV5
Plant: Visakhapatnam Fertilizer Complex (VFC)
Area: Ammonia Synthesis Loop — Area 3
Hazardous Material: Ammonia (NH3) | CAS: 7664-41-7
Issue Date: 15-December-2024
Approved by: Plant Safety Officer K. Prasad

1. SCOPE
This SOP governs emergency response procedures for all personnel in Area 3
in the event of NH3 release, fire, explosion, or other process safety event.
All shifts must conduct quarterly desktop drills on this procedure per
Factory Act 1948 emergency preparedness requirement.

2. NH3 RELEASE EMERGENCY LEVELS
Level 1 — ALERT (25–49 ppm NH3 detected):
- Notify Control Room immediately
- Shift Supervisor evaluates source and determines corrective action
- All hot work permits in Area 3 must be suspended immediately
- Non-essential personnel leave Area 3
- Emergency response team on standby

Level 2 — ALARM (50–99 ppm NH3 detected):
- Activate evacuation alarm (continuous siren)
- Mandatory evacuation of Area 3 to assembly point (Parking Lot C)
- Emergency response team enters Area 3 with SCBA (Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus)
- Fire brigade notified
- Wind sock checked — evacuate downwind personnel from adjacent areas
- NH3 water curtains activated (manual valve MV-A3-WC01)

Level 3 — EMERGENCY (≥100 ppm NH3 detected or visible plume):
- Activate plant-wide emergency siren (pulsating)
- Full plant evacuation ordered
- External emergency services notified (Fire Brigade, District Emergency Officer)
- Emergency Operations Center (EOC) activated
- Public alert via plant loudspeaker and SMS to nearby villages (within 1 km radius)
- Isolate all NH3 sources — emergency shutdown of ammonia synthesis loop
- Deploy foam blankets if NH3 plume near flammable material zones

3. EQUIPMENT FAILURE EMERGENCY (HE-301 SPECIFIC)
If HE-301 catastrophic failure (shell rupture, massive flange leak):
- Activate Level 2 or Level 3 response based on GD-303 reading
- Close isolation valves XV-301A (shell side) and XV-301B (tube side)
- Open emergency vent EV-301 to route NH3 to flare (control room activates remotely)
- Do not attempt manual valve operation if NH3 concentration >25 ppm without SCBA
- HE-301 is in direct line-of-sight from Control Room — visual confirmation mandatory

4. EVACUATION ROUTES
Primary Route: Main access road east toward admin building → Assembly Point Parking Lot C
Secondary Route: Emergency exit west toward perimeter fence gate G-3 → Assembly Point Fire Station

CRITICAL: Move perpendicular to wind direction, not downwind. Check wind sock
at Area 3 entrance before evacuating.

5. PERSONNEL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
All personnel in Area 3 must have personal NH3 escape respirator within 10 meters
at all times. Escape respirators located at ER-A3-01, ER-A3-02, ER-A3-03 (marked
with green strobe lights). SCBA required for entry into NH3 concentration >25 ppm.

7. POST-EMERGENCY ACTIONS
All Level 2 and Level 3 events require:
- Incident investigation per IR-TEMPLATE-001 within 24 hours
- DISH notification within 48 hours per Factory Act Section 88A
- Root cause analysis within 7 days
- Public report to District Magistrate if external evacuation triggered

8. TRAINING AND DRILL REQUIREMENTS
- Annual full-scale NH3 release drill (all shifts participate)
- Quarterly desktop emergency drill
- Monthly check of emergency equipment (SCBA, escape respirators, water curtains, GD-303)
- New hires must complete emergency response training within 7 days of joining
